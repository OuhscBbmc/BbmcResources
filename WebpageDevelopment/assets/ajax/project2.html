MIECHV Evaluations
OUHSC is conducting an external evaluation of the OK State Competitive MIECHV grant. We refer to this project as "Giving Parents a Voice" since we are reaching out to families asking how best to service their unique needs.

This is a mixed-methods project with extensive quantitative and qualitative data collection and analysis. The quantitative portion involves a longitudinal survey with families in five targeted counties. The data collected from this survey address all MIECHV benchmarks, ACEs, and service utilization. This data will act as a comparison group for Evidence-Based Home Visiting clients. The qualitative portion of the study involves semi-structured one-on-one interviews with Evidence-Based Home Visiting providers, administrators, and legislators, as well as focus groups involving families eligible for these services.

Dynamic reports showing the results of data analysis for this project may be found at our MReporting site.

The MIECHV Expansion project includes another community survey targeted at EBHV clients, as well as a pilot EEG study with Dr. Lauren Ethridge, director of University of Oklahoma's Brain and Biomarker Laboratory. For the pilot study, EBHV-eligible children and caregivers will be administered an EEG. This study aims to evaluate how families with different risk factors react to emotional stimuli.
